Why do you want to be a final presenter? I have felt a strong connection to our product since its conception, and would relish the opportunity to convey my passion for and knowledge about the product to the public.

Presenting on Rho in our final presentation would be a truly fulfilling closeout to the arduous product development process that we have undertaken, and I believe I could do it well (and enjoy it!).

What went well in your prior 2.009 presentations? I received a lot of positive feedback from reviewers about my recovery from a stall during Sketch Model review, and think that that recovery demonstrates ability to think on my feet and appear personable during presentations. From that experience I learned the importance of developing talks around general points rather than a memorized script.

I subsequently improved my presentation preparation and was able to speak clearly about design features and motivations during Assembly Review. I also received positive feedback on my explanations during Mockup Review demonstrations, where I effectively communicated our learnings and paths forward to our reviewers. During Technical Review, I clearly articulated our product's value and its differentiation from conventional BCDs with minimal preparation.

I also think that in general while presenting I project my voice effectively and enunciate so that my words can be heard clearly by audiences.

What do you wish you could have improved in your prior 2.009 presentations? I wish I hadn't frozen during Sketch Model presentations, and wish I hadn't tried to memorize a script. I also think I could have spoken at a slightly slower pace during Assembly Review when addressing our UI.

Which of your contributions to Rho are you most proud of, and why? I am proud of my overall investment in and work on the product and its many component iterations, and am specifically proud of my organized and broad-viewed performance as a task force lead for bcd-integration leading up to Assembly Review, when our task force executed an impressive series of tasks under a short timeline.
